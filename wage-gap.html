Closing the Wage Gap

Why should you care?

Are you a working professional who champions gender equality and economic opportunity? The proportion of women among skilled professionals continues to increase in today's labor workforce.

But the data shows us that income disparities by gender persist across sectors and levels of educational attainment.

Women worldwide are paid less for their labor

The United States has among the highest gender wage gaps in the world. A common misbelief is that pay inequality is a problem unique to developing economies. The data tells us otherwise.

Women in every U.S. state experience pay inequity

The gender wage gap is not bound by cultural landscape or political lines. This heatmap of U.S. states shows us income inequality by gender is ubiquitous across the country.

Median weekly earnings of full-time and salary workers by state, as compared to state's presidential election in 2020

Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics, The American Presidency Project at the University of California, Santa Cruz

Women across all levels of educational attainment experience pay inequity

A story people tell themselves is that the women who are being paid less than men must not be as educated. This is q problematic line of thinking in and of itself, but moreover, the data shows us quite the opposite. Gender wage gaps widen as educational attainment advances.

Source: U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics
