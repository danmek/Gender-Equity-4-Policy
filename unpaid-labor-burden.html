The burden of “invisible labor”

Why should you care?

  • Are you a woman balancing a career and a household - or do you love someone who fits that description?
  • In the last few decades, more and more women have entered the workforce. But someone is still doing the dishes, cooking, and taking care of kids.
    • Women are still spending more time on household activities, and more time on work total.
    • This creates a toll for women all over the world.
  • Women spend more time on unpaid labor and total work:
    • Across the world
    • Even if they are employed
    • Even if they have a present spouse

Women spend more time on unpaid work across the world

Time spent on unpaid work by gender, OECD data, 2022

  • The time women spend on unpaid work is almost double than the time men spend on unpaid work on average across the world.
  • In all OECD countries, women spend more time on unpaid work than men.

Sourse: OECD website

Women spend more time on total work across the world

Total time spent working by gender, OECD data, 2022

  • The total work hours, paid + unpaid, of women is higher than men, all over the world (except Mexico).

Sourse: OECD website

Women have a higher household burden, even if they are full-employed

Time spent on households by gender by employment status in the U.S., 2019

  • Women employed full-time in the U.S spend more time in household activities than men employed full-time, and also helping and caring for other household members.

Sourse: U.S. BUREAU OF LABOR STATISTICS Website

Women spend more time on all time-demanding household activities

Time spent on households per activity, by gender in the U.S., 2019

  • Women in the U.S spend 50% more time in household activities than men.
  • The only household activities which men spend more time on are the shorter traditionally mail gender activities.
